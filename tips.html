Tips for Strong Passwords

Use these guidelines to create passwords and passphrases that are much harder to crack while still being memorable.

General guidelines

  • Use at least 12â€“16 characters whenever possible.
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.
  • Avoid personal information like your name, birthday, or phone number.
  • Do not reuse the same password across multiple accounts.
  • Consider using a passphrase made from random words.

Passphrase idea

Start with a sentence or phrase you can remember and then add numbers and symbols to it, for example a line from a song or a quote turned into a pattern.

Weak password patterns to avoid

  • Simple sequences like 123456, abcdef, or qwerty.
  • Common words like password or admin.
  • Passwords that just add ! or 123 to the end of a weak word.

Use a password manager

A password manager can generate and store long, random passwords for you so that you only need to remember one strong master password.
